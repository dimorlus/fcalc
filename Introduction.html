Introduction
Input for this calculator are normal C expressions containing operators, float or integer constants, variables. Precedence and semantic of operators is the same as in C language. Variables and functions names are case insensitive. Following functions and operators are supported:
Functions: Operators:
arccos(x), acos(x) ArcCos in radians Common
arcsin(x), asin(x) ArcSin in radians ++ Increment -- Decrement
arctg(x), atan(x) ArcTg in radians + Plus (also for string) - Minus
sing(x) Sin of degrees ! Not ~ Complement
sin(x) Sin of radians * Multiplication / Division
cosg(x) Cos of degrees % Remainder ** Power
cos(x) Cos of radian && Logical AND & AND
ctgg(x) Ctg of degrees || Logical OR | OR
ctg(x) Ctg of radian << Shift left >> Shift right
tgg(x) Tg of degrees >>> Unsign shift right # XOR
tg(x), tan(x) Tg of radian < Less > Greater
sinh(x), sh(x) Hyperbolic sine != Not equal <> Not equal
cosh(x), ch(x) Hyperbolic cosine += Increase -= Decrease
tanh(x), th(x) Hyperbolic tangent *= Mul and assign /= Div and assign
atan2(y,x) Arc tangent of y/x. %= Rem and assign **= Pow and assign
sqrt(x), root2(x) Square root <<= Shift and assign >>= Shift and assign
lg(x), log10(x) Logarithm base 10, Log10(x) #= XOR and assign &= AND and assign
ln(x), log(x) Natural logarithm ^= XOR and assign >>>= Shift and assign
exp10(x) 10x 'Pascal' - style
exp(x) Exponent, ex := Assign = Compare
abs(x) Absolute value ^ Power // Parallel resistors
not(n) Inversion all bits, ~n 'C' - style
round(x) Round value = Assign == Compare
frac(x) Returns the fractional part ^ XOR // Parallel resistors
float(n) Convert to float First using of '==' switches engine to 'C'-style syntax. First using of ':=' switches engine to 'Pas'-style syntax. Default is 'Pas'-style syntax.
int(x) Convert to int
prime(n) Smallest prime number
root3(x), cbrt(x) Cube root, x1/3 printf(fmt, ...) C-style printf. Result in the string value.
rootn(x,n) nth root, x1/n fprn(fmt, ...)
